Climate & Culture Survey Results

Released Publicly  -  April, 2026
Section 1

Understanding the Context Behind the Data #

The educators of ORCSD care deeply about our students, our schools, and the communities we serve. Our dedication is unwavering. Our commitment to maintaining high-quality, safe, and joyful learning environments remains as strong as ever.

We advocate for:

  • Open and transparent communication between the Guild and the School Board
  • Meaningful and collaborative educator involvement in decisions that directly impact teaching and learning
  • Full adherence to our legally binding collective bargaining agreement

A grievance represents a formal acknowledgment of a contract violation. It is not pursued lightly and occurs only after good faith efforts to resolve concerns collaboratively between the Guild and administration have been exhausted.

The filing of more than four grievances in less than two years, compared to just one filed over the previous three decades, represents a significant and concerning shift. This pattern reflects a breakdown in respect for agreed-upon working conditions and professional boundaries, with a measurable negative impact on the professional climate within our district.

Section 2

Context & Timeline #

This work began prior to any budget, staffing, or reduction-in-force discussions and is rooted in broader concerns related to leadership, communication, collaboration, and workplace culture.

  • Fall 2025: Guild membership (200+ members) directed the Executive Board to conduct a climate and culture survey in partnership with NEA-NH
  • December 2025: Anonymous survey administered to Guild members
  • January 2026: Results shared with membership, who requested presentation to the School Board
  • February 2026: Survey prepared for public release; at the Board's request, the Guild paused publication to allow for direct Board review
  • March 5, 2026: Survey results presented to the full School Board in non-public session, along with requests for:
    • Consideration of the data in Superintendent contract renewal discussions
    • Increased transparency and direct communication between the Board and staff
  • Since March 5, 2026: Two additional School Board meetings have occurred without clear communication regarding next steps
  • April 2026: At the direction of over 200 members, the Guild Executive Board is proceeding with the public release of Superintendent-related survey data.

Section 11

Closing #

ORCSD educators will continue to support students with professionalism, care, and dedication. At the same time, we believe it is essential that the concerns reflected here are acknowledged and addressed so that our schools remain strong, supportive places for everyone.

A defining strength of the Oyster River Cooperative School District is its commitment to collaborative dialogue, professional discourse, and reflective practice. Upholding that standard is critical as we move forward together.
